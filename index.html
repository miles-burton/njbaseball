Stat Glossary
Definitions and formulas for every metric on Diamond Index
Hitting
PA
Plate Appearances
Total times a batter completes a turn at bat. AB + BB + HBP + SF + SH.
AVG
Batting Average
H / AB. The most basic measure of hitting performance.
OBP
On-Base Percentage
(H + BB + HBP) / (AB + BB + HBP + SF). How often a batter reaches base per plate appearance.
SLG
Slugging Percentage
Total bases / AB. Measures the power component of hitting.
OPS
On-Base Plus Slugging
OBP + SLG. A quick composite of a hitter's ability to get on base and hit for power.
ISO
Isolated Power
SLG − AVG. Measures raw power by isolating extra bases from hits.
BB%
Walk Percentage
BB / PA × 100. Measures plate discipline; how often a batter draws a walk.
BsR
Baserunning Runs
(SB × 0.2) + (3B × 0.1). Estimates run value added through baserunning.
wOBA
Weighted On-Base Average
(0.7×(BB+HBP) + 0.9×1B + 1.3×2B + 1.6×3B + 2.0×HR) / PA. Assigns run values to each offensive event. League average ≈ .353.
wRAA
Weighted Runs Above Average
((wOBA − .353) / 1.12) × PA. Runs contributed above or below a league-average hitter.
wRC
Weighted Runs Created
wRAA + (PA × .177). Total estimated runs created by the player.
wRC+
Weighted Runs Created Plus
((wOBA − .353) / 1.12 + .177) / .177 × 100. Park- and league-adjusted offensive production. 100 = league average. 150 = 50% above average.
PS
Player Score
0-100 composite score from qualified-player percentiles. Hitting PS weights wRC+ 45%, wOBA 20%, OPS 15%, ISO 10%, BB% 5%, and BsR 5%.
Pitching
IP
Innings Pitched
Total innings pitched. Partial innings shown as decimals (e.g. 6.1 = 6⅓ innings).
ERA
Earned Run Average
(ER / IP) × 7. Earned runs allowed per seven innings (high school game length). Lower is better.
FIP
Fielding Independent Pitching
((3×(BB+HB) − 2×K) / IP) + 3.10. Estimates ERA based only on outcomes the pitcher controls: strikeouts, walks, and hit batters. Removes defense and luck.
WHIP
Walks + Hits per IP
(BB + H) / IP. Measures baserunners allowed per inning. League average ≈ 1.30.
K/7
Strikeouts per 7 Innings
(K / IP) × 7. Strikeout rate normalized to a standard high school game length.
BB/7
Walks per 7 Innings
(BB / IP) × 7. Walk rate normalized to 7 innings. Lower is better.
K/BB
Strikeout-to-Walk Ratio
K / BB. The single best indicator of a pitcher's command. 2.0+ is solid; 3.0+ is elite.
ERA−
ERA Minus
(ERA / 4.31) × 100. ERA relative to conference average. 100 = average. Below 100 = better than average.
FIP−
FIP Minus
(FIP / 4.31) × 100. FIP relative to conference average. 100 = average. Below 100 = better than average.
RAA
Runs Above Average
((4.31 − ERA) / 7) × IP. Runs saved compared to a league-average pitcher over the same innings.
PS
Pitcher Player Score
0-100 composite score from qualified-pitcher percentiles. Pitching PS weights ERA- 30%, FIP- 25%, WHIP 15%, K/7 15%, K/BB 10%, and IP 5%.
Standings & Team Metrics
PCT
Win Percentage
W / (W + L). Standard winning percentage.
RDIFF
Run Differential
RS − RA. The total difference between runs scored and runs allowed. One of the strongest predictors of future performance.
RS/G
Runs Scored per Game
Total runs scored divided by games played. Measures offensive output.
RA/G
Runs Allowed per Game
Total runs allowed divided by games played. Measures pitching and defensive performance.
PYTH
Pythagorean Expected Record
RS^1.83 / (RS^1.83 + RA^1.83) × G. Estimates the win total a team deserves based on runs scored and allowed. Teams significantly above their Pythagorean record have likely been lucky in close games.
Power Rankings
DI SCORE
Diamond Index Team Score
0-100 predictive rating based on adjusted offensive and defensive efficiency. It is converted from Pythagorean expected win percentage using a baseball exponent of 1.83.
AdjO
Adjusted Offense
Runs scored per game adjusted for opponent defensive strength. It estimates how many runs the team would score against an average opponent.
AdjD
Adjusted Defense
Runs allowed per game adjusted for opponent offensive strength. Lower is better. It estimates how many runs the team would allow against an average opponent.
SOS
Strength of Schedule
Average opponent quality, calculated from opponents' adjusted offensive and defensive efficiencies and converted to a Pythagorean rating.
Luck
Record Luck
Actual win percentage minus expected win percentage. Positive means the team has won more than its adjusted run profile predicts; negative means the opposite.
BLOW
Blowout Margin Dampening
Average runs per game removed by the blowout dampener before ratings are calculated. Margins of 6 or fewer are unchanged; larger margins are compressed.
Method
Adjusted Efficiency Loop
Each game is adjusted for opponent quality, then team ratings are recalculated repeatedly until offense, defense, and schedule strength stabilize.
Percentile Colors
Gold (90th+) — Elite. Top 10% of all qualified players.
Dark Gold (75–89th) — Above average. Solid contributor.
Brown (58–74th) — Slightly above average.
Slate (42–57th) — League average.
Blue (25–41st) — Below average.
Dark Blue (0–24th) — Well below average.
Percentiles are calculated against qualified players only (50+ PA for hitters, 15+ IP for pitchers).
